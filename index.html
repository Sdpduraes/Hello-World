Nelson Mandela

The First black president of South Africa.

Nelson Mandela at a benefit for the Mandela Children’s Fund — Cape Town.

Important facts about Nelson Mandela:

  • 1918 - July 18: Rolihlahla Dalibhunga Mandela is born a member of the Madiba clan. His tribal name, "Rolihalah," means "troublemaker." He is later given his English name, Nelson, by a teacher at his school.
  • 1927- His father dies. The acting chief of the Thembu clan, Jongintaba Dalindyebo becomes his guardian and ensures he receives an excellent education.
  • 1939- Moves to Johannesburg to escape an arranged marriage and experiences the system of apartheid which forbade the black population to vote, travel without permission or own land. Completed his degree via a correspondence course at the University of South Africa Studies Law at the University of Witwatersrand whilst living in Alexandra
  • 1943- Joins the African National Congress (ANC) as an activist.
  • 1944- Forms the Youth League of the ANC with Oliver Tambo and Walter Sislu.
  • 1952- Opens the first black legal firm in South Africa.
  • 1962- August 5: Arrested after living on the run for seventeen months and was imprisoned in the Johannesburg Fort. October 25: Nelson Mandela was sentenced to five years in prison but again goes on the run.
  • 1964- June 12: Captured and convicted of sabotage and treason and sentenced to life imprisonment at the age of 46, initially on Robben island where he would be kept for 18 years.
  • 1990- February 11: Nelson Mandela is released from prison after 27 years.
  • 1991- Becomes President of the African National Congress ( ANC ).
  • 1993- Nelson Mandela is awarded the Nobel Peace Prize.
  • 1994- April 26: Free Elections where black South Africans are allowed to vote for the first time. Nelson Mandela turns out to be the first black President of South Africa.
  • 1998- Marries Graca Machel, the widow of the former president of Mozambique, on his 80th birthday.
  • 2000- Appointed as mediator in the civil war in Burundi.
  • 2003- Attacked the foreign policy of the George W. Bush.
  • 2004 -June: Nelson Mandela announced that he would be retiring from public life at the age of 85.
  • 2013- dies aged 95.

Nelson Mandela became the first black president of South Africa in 1994, serving until 1999. A symbol of global peacemaking, he won the Nobel Peace Prize in 1993.
